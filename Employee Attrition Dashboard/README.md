# Employee Attrition Analysis Dashboard (1-Page Executive Summary)

An interactive, single-page Power BI dashboard designed to analyze employee attrition, identify key turnover drivers, and provide immediate workforce insights. This high-level executive summary helps HR teams and managers understand employee retention patterns across demographics, education levels, salary bands, and job roles.

## 🚀 Key Features & Insights

This dashboard isolates workforce KPIs and attrition trends using key operational dimensions:

### 📊 Executive Summary KPIs
*   **Total Employees:** `446` total active headcount analyzed.
*   **Attrition Count:** `92` employees have left the company.
*   **Attrition Rate:** `20.6%` overall turnover rate.
*   **Average Age:** `37` years old.
*   **Average Salary:** `$7.0K`.
*   **Average Tenure:** `7.3` years at the company.

### 📈 Turnover & Retention Analysis
*   **Attrition by Gender:** Treemap showcasing attrition distribution between Male (`50` exits) and Female (`35` exits) employees.
*   **Attrition by Education:** Donut chart highlighting that employees with backgrounds in **Marketing (38%)** and **Life Sciences (32%)** make up the largest share of departures.
*   **Attrition by Age Group:** Column chart revealing that turnover is highest in young professionals aged **26–35** (`41` exits), followed by the **18–25** bracket (`18` exits).
*   **Salary & Compensation Impact:** Horizontal bar chart displaying that the lowest salary band (**Upto 5k**) bears the highest attrition rate with `43` departures, signaling a strong correlation between pay scale and retention.
*   **Tenure Tracking (Years at Company):** An area chart charting employee departures over time, showing a massive peak in turnover at the **1-year** mark (`17` exits).
*   **Job Role Breakdown:** Highlights that **Sales Executives** (`57` exits) and **Sales Representatives** (`33` exits) experience the highest organizational churn.
